✦ About Leh-Ladakh

Leh-Ladakh — An Overview

Leh-Ladakh sits at the roof of the world, a high-altitude desert in the northernmost tip of India where the Himalayas and Karakoram ranges converge. At elevations between 3,000–5,600 metres, this remote moonscape of barren mountains, crystal rivers, and jewel-like lakes is unlike anywhere else on Earth.

Ancient Buddhist monasteries cling to cliffsides, prayer flags flutter in the thin mountain air, and traditional Ladakhi villages offer a window into a way of life barely changed for centuries. The legendary Pangong Tso — a lake whose colour shifts from turquoise to sapphire — and the world's highest motorable roads make Ladakh a bucket-list destination for adventurers and seekers alike.

✦ Highlights

Top Experiences in Leh-Ladakh

🏔️
Pangong Tso Lake
The iconic high-altitude lake that changes colour from turquoise to blue to silver — best experienced at sunrise and sunset.
🛣️
World's Highest Roads
Drive the legendary Khardung La pass (5,359m) and Chang La — experiences that every road traveller dreams of.
🙏
Ancient Monasteries
Visit Thiksey, Hemis, Diskit, and Lamayuru monasteries — ancient centres of Tibetan Buddhist culture and art.
🏕️
Nubra Valley Camping
Camp in the Nubra Valley's sand dunes, ride a double-humped Bactrian camel, and watch a sky full of stars.
🏄
Zanskar River Rafting
White-water raft through the dramatic gorges of the Zanskar River — one of India's most thrilling rafting routes.
🌌
Stargazing
Ladakh's thin, clear atmosphere makes it one of the best stargazing destinations in Asia — the Milky Way is visible to the naked eye.
