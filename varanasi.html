✦ About Varanasi

Varanasi — An Overview

Varanasi, known as Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on Earth and the spiritual capital of India. Located on the banks of the sacred River Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, this ancient city has drawn pilgrims, philosophers, and seekers for over 3,000 years.

The 88 ghats that line the riverfront are Varanasi's living pulse — each morning they host ablutions, yoga, flower offerings, and the timeless rhythm of a city that has never changed. The evening Ganga Aarti, a fire ceremony held at Dashashwamedh Ghat, is among the most mesmerising spectacles anywhere in the world. Varanasi is also famous for its Banarasi silk, classical music, and the nearby deer park at Sarnath where Buddha delivered his first sermon.

✦ Highlights

Top Experiences in Varanasi

🙏
Ganga Aarti
The nightly fire ritual at Dashashwamedh Ghat, performed by priests in perfect synchrony, is a mesmerising blend of devotion, music, and light.
🚣
Sunrise Boat Ride
A gentle rowing boat on the Ganges at dawn is one of India's most transcendent experiences — watch the holy city awaken.
🏛️
Kashi Vishwanath Temple
One of the 12 Jyotirlingas and the most sacred Shiva temple in India, recently renovated with a spectacular corridor.
🦌
Sarnath
Just 10 km from Varanasi, where Buddha gave his first sermon — visit the Dhamekh Stupa and the excellent archaeological museum.
🧵
Banarasi Silk
Shop for legendary Banarasi silk sarees and brocades directly from weavers in the old city's narrow lanes.
🎵
Classical Music
Varanasi is the cradle of Hindustani classical music — attend a late-night baithak in the lanes of the old city.
