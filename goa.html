✦ About Goa

Goa — An Overview

Goa, India's smallest state, punches far above its size in personality. This former Portuguese colony on India's west coast blends colonial architecture, tropical beaches, spiced seafood, and a vibrant arts scene into an experience unlike any other in India.

North Goa thrills with its beach shacks, flea markets, and nightlife at Baga and Calangute, while South Goa enchants with pristine, tranquil shores like Palolem and Agonda. Beyond the beaches, Goa rewards the curious explorer with 16th-century cathedrals, spice plantations, old Portuguese mansions, and a fascinating multi-layered cultural identity.

✦ Highlights

Top Experiences in Goa

🏖️
Beaches of Goa
From the lively shores of Baga and Anjuna in the north to the serene Palolem and Agonda in the south — each beach has its own character.
⛵
Water Sports
Parasailing, jet skiing, scuba diving, windsurfing, and dolphin-watching cruises are all easily available along the coastline.
🏛️
Old Goa Heritage
Explore the magnificent Basilica of Bom Jesus (UNESCO) and Se Cathedral, remnants of Goa's grand Portuguese era.
🌶️
Goan Cuisine
Indulge in fish curries, prawn balchão, pork vindaloo, and bebinca — a layered dessert unique to Goa.
🌿
Spice Plantation
Tour a fragrant spice plantation in Ponda — see cardamom, nutmeg, vanilla and pepper growing in their natural habitat.
🎶
Flea Markets & Nightlife
The Saturday Night Market at Arpora and Anjuna Flea Market are legendary for crafts, music, and global street food.
